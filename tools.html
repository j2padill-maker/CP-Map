Formula Reference
▶
To find amperage from a millivolt drop reading:
Amps = mV reading × pipe constant ÷ footage (riser to riser)
▶
Thin wall pipe carries less material cross-section, resulting in lower resistance and a lower constant. Always confirm wall type before selecting constant — using the wrong one will miscalculate current by 40–45% on 4" and 6" pipe.

CP Protection Criteria (CSE Reference)
✓
NACE criterion 1 — instant-off potential at or more negative than:
−850 mV vs CSE (most common criterion)

NACE criterion 2 — 100 mV minimum polarization shift (on vs instant-off):
|OFF − ON| ≥ 100 mV

To Find Voltage (E)

E =I × R
E =P ÷ I
E =√(P × R)

To Find Current (I)

I =E ÷ R
I =P ÷ E
I =√(P ÷ R)

To Find Resistance (R)

R =E ÷ I
R =E² ÷ P
R =P ÷ I²

To Find Power (P)

P =E × I
P =E² ÷ R
P =I² × R

📏Useful Field Formulas
Geometry and unit conversion reference — from Farwest Corrosion Control field card
1
To calculate the circumference of a circle, multiply the diameter by 3.1416.
C = d × 3.1416
2
To calculate the area of a circle, multiply 3.1416 by the square of the radius.
A = 3.1416 × r²
3
To calculate the area of a triangle, multiply the base by ½ the perpendicular height.
A = base × (height ÷ 2)
4
To calculate the area of a cylinder, multiply the circumference by the length.
A = (d × 3.1416) × length
5
To calculate the volume of a cylinder, multiply the area of the end by the length.
V = (3.1416 × r²) × length
6
A gallon of water weighs 8.25 pounds and contains 231 cubic inches.
1 gal = 8.25 lb = 231 in³ = 7.48 gal/ft³
7
A cubic foot of water contains 7.48 gallons and weighs 62.5 pounds. Contains 1728 cubic inches.
1 ft³ = 7.48 gal = 62.5 lb = 1728 in³
